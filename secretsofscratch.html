Secrets of Scratch

Author and Illustrator | Sep 2019 - Sep 2020

Overview

"Secrets of Scratch" is a book I created to share my love for making games with Scratch! It started as a school project in 2019 and was completed and published in Taiwan in 2020.

People

Author, Illustrator: Enoch Kang
Editor: April Chen

Passion Project

The project started as a school project. We were given a year to create whatever "Passion Project" we wanted to. Creative freedom and schoolwork don't usually fit in the same sentence so I knew I had to make the most out of this opportunity! I started developing the book in 2019, and the COVID pandemic was just around the corner. With everyone stuck in online classes, I had a great window to focus all my time into completing this book! Looking back, the 2020 lockdown was probably what made this project happen so quickly and in the way that it did.
This is a really old project so I don't have too many process photos from that time! I do, however, have a video I created when the book first got published so feel free to check it out if you'd like more information!

Some of my favorite details

There are comics about programming littered throughout the book

The comic-in-the-middle-of-a-book idea was inspired by my childhood favorites: Captain Underpants by Dav Pilkey and Big Nate by Lincoln Peirce (specifically Big Nate Strikes Again, where Nate makes his own almanac). I took inspiration from my own experiences as a programmer and created these lighthearted segments to make the book feel more personal!

There is a flipbook in the corner of the book!

I drew the animation in Krita and placed it in frame by frame into inDesign. The animation is of a little stick figure running and leveling up with progressively cooler gear towards the end of the book. The color of the gear and progression of our stick figure hero is matching with each section of the book. As the tutorials get more advanced your little stick figure companion also levels up with you.

The book can be found at public libraries!

In addition to being available online and in bookstores, I recently found out that my book is actually available for kids to read for free via public libraries in Taiwan! I love libraries so much and knowing that my work has somehow found its way into libraries just warms my heart and gives me so much motivation to keep going!
