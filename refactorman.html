Overview

Refactorman is a character I created inspired by the comics and books I loved growing up. This project acts as a great creative outlet whenever I start to feel overwhelmed by the cold and calculated world of engineering. So far there are 2 books and a cast of colorful characters that I enjoy drawing very much. This project is a great place to work on my storytelling and drawing skills! Origins

Refactorman started as a silly doodle I drew during a particularly long and boring Software Engineering lecture. We were talking about rapid prototyping and SCRUM development practices when I had the idea for a Captain Underpants-like character that runs around solving programming problems all day.
The second crucial ingredient was the the daily struggles as a programmers that my friends and I face. I recally Bryan (a close friend who works with me on projects) and I were at a game developer gathering and he was talking about his current project at work and how difficult it was to pick up someone else's project without proper documentation. His dramatic retelling of his work story was so memorable that I thought it would be a great idea for a short comic - that's what inspired the plot for the first book!
