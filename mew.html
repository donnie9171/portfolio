MEW

Overview

MEW stands for Multi-agent Exploration Workbench. It is a software I am developing to help us explore the curious overlap of AI and education.

People

Main Developer: Enoch Kang
Supervisor: Dr. TW Chim

What is MEW

MEW is an attempt to turn complicated AI into simple building materials (nodes) for beginners to make projects with, the same way Scratch turned abstract code into tangible building blocks. The goal is to lower the barrier of entry for making simple AI projects in hopes of the hands on experience helping people develop an intuition about how AI systems work in the real world. The node based approach is largely inspired by the tldraw computer project by the tldraw team.

What will I be exploring?

Software Design

What interface will be the most intuitive for beginners to tinker with AI (nodes or blocks)?
What are the challenges and how can we deisgn solutions in our software to reduce friction and break down barriers to entry?

Workshop Material Design

Just like the Matchbox project, having a stable and consistent base system is only half of the task. What are the unique projects and workshops we can do with this new software and materials? Most importantly can we make anything fun with it? How do we design lesson plans and tutorial materials that can play to the strengths of this system and introduce the technology to the kids in a fun and sincere way?

Real World Implications

How much will it cost to operate? Are there any security risks? Adversarial prompting? Attacks? Ethics and safety? Developing and testing the software will give me many opportunities to experiment and observe how a system like this would operate in a real classroom. How real people will react when put in front of a technology like this.
